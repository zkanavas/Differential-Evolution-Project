These results show that as the recombination constant, CR, increases, the runtime decreases. There were no significant changes in accuracy with changing recombination constant. However, in general, experiments with lower recombination constants selected a greater number of structural attributes.

As the recombination constant increases the algorithm is more likely to take elements from the mutated vector. As the recombination constant decreases the algorithm is more likely to take elements from the best current solution vector. This is why we generally see more structural attributes incorporated for lower recombination constants and vis versa, as the inital conditions with the highest accuracies have less structural attributes incorporated.

Note: this relationship between recombination constant value & number of incorporated structural attributes may change with different inital conditions.
